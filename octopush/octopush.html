Game play:

The game is played by two teams of up to ten players, with six players in the water at one time and the others substituting continuously during play. The object of the game is to manoeuvre the puck into your opponent's goal. At the start of each game the puck is placed in the middle of the pool and the teams start on opposite walls. On the referee's signal the game begins. As soon as the game begins the teams will swim to the puck, the forwards attempting to gain possession for their team. The team then work together to try and score a goal while defending their own goal. Once a goal has been scored the puck is put back in the middle and the teams return to their respective starting points and play commences as before. Full details of the rules can be found here.

History of the Sport:

The sport was named Octopush as the team size was originally 8. It was started by Alan Blake and other BSAC divers in Southsea in 1954.

You can find more details at GBUWH.co.uk

Equipment used:

Players wear a diving mask, fins, and a snorkel for play.

The stick ("Pusher"), including handle, is about 350mm long and is coloured white or black to indicate the player's team.

Safety gear includes a water polo style cap (to protect the outer and, more importantly, inner ear) a mouthguard, and a glove for the playing hand (to protect against pool-bottom abrasion and, in some designs, knuckle protection against puck impact).

The goals ("Gulls") are troughs three metres in width and lie at opposite ends of the playing area.

The puck ("Squid") is approximately the size of an ice hockey puck but is made of plastic-coated lead or similar material, weighing about 1.3Kg. The puck's weight brings it to rest on the pool bottom, though it can be lifted in passes ("flicks").
